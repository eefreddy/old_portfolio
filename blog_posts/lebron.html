Do we have to be in 2030 to appreciate Lebron James?

How does rosy retrospection play a part in sports?


Posted on June 21, 2017

I don’t think humanity as a species appreciates the present. We either gaze longingly upon the past or lovingly towards the future. We yearn for that one elapsed moment, that one lost object or in this case a retired athlete (or a dead celebrity for that matter). In sports we revere our players as gods (see: Michael Jordan) but only after they retire. However, these poor fellas are crucified during their playing careers. Why?

Rosy Retrospection

I think this is because of a psychological phenomenon (a cognitive bias) called “Rosy retrospection”. We tend to remember events of the past more positively than they actually were. We judge the past disproportionately more positively than the events of the present. This is probably why that one burger place never tastes as good as you remembered or why we find that one activity is never as fun as the second time around.

We have to be in 2030 to realize how amazing Lebron is compared to players like Michael Jordan* simply because we look at Jordan through the rosy retrospective. Even after just one season we have started the process of immortalizing Kobe Bryant who wasn't the most liked player during his career to say the least. I suspect that when Lebron retires in about 5 or 6 years (prove me wrong please), we will forget his flaws and remember him beating a team that won 72 games and was up 3-1 on him. Hopefully we will remember him leading his team from down 3-0 the very next year (Game 5 is tomorrow).

Okay, let’s zoom out for a second. Outside sports why is it that we don't appreciate certain events that happen to us as much as we appreciate it later on? Is this a good thing or a bad thing? I look more fondly upon my hiking adventures now than a year ago when I was trudging through knee deep snow. However, you have to appreciate each situation you are in because no two situations are similar. Once you realize that you’ll never have the same experience again you start to appreciate things more in realtime. So let’s just sit back and enjoy our journeys in real time and in retrospection.
